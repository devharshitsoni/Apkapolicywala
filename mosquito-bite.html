Who Can Get Mosquito Bite Insurance?

While Aapka Policywala offers a variety of mosquito bite insurance options to its customers, one needs to understand there are certain criteria in order to apply for the purchase or renewal of this type of policy. Here’s a breakdown of who can qualify for coverage:

Family-Friendly Coverage

This policy offers protection for your entire family, guaranteeing everyone you care about is covered. This includes yourself, your spouse, dependent children (from newborns!), and even dependent parents.

Age Eligibility

The minimum entry age for the proposer (the policyholder), spouse, and dependent parents is 18 years. The maximum entry age is 65 years for everyone except dependent children. There’s no upper age limit for dependent children, Meaning, you can make certain that even your little ones are covered from the very beginning.

What Does Mosquito Bite Insurance Cover?

Just like a sunscreen cream protects you from the scorching sun rays, mos bite insurance policy on our platform will protect you from the financial burden of vector-borne diseases. You can also think of it as a suit of armor against a range of illnesses transmitted by mosquitoes, offering various coverage options to fit your needs. Here are the specific diseases covered by the M-Care policy:

Number Features Benefits
1 Dengue Fever This mosquito-borne illness can cause flu-like symptoms and sometimes develop into a more serious form.
2 Malaria Another serious mosquito-borne disease. Malaria can cause fever, chills, and even life-threatening complications.
3 Filariasis (Payable Only Once in a Lifetime) This parasitic infection can cause swelling and other issues. The mosquito bite care policy offers a one-time payout for this condition.
4 Kala Azar This potentially fatal disease can cause fever, fatigue, and enlargement of the spleen and liver.
5 Chikungunya This mosquito-borne virus can cause fever, joint pain, and headaches.
6 Japanese Encephalitis This mosquito-borne disease can cause inflammation of the brain. It can lead to serious neurological issues.

What's Not Covered by Mosquito Bite Insurance?

Just like inclusions, it’s important to understand what the mosquito bite insurance policy offered at Aapka Policywala’s platform doesn’t cover:

Number Features Benefits
1 Non-Vector Borne Diseases The policy is specifically designed for vector-borne diseases transmitted by mosquitoes. Any treatment for other medical conditions won’t be covered.
2 Short Hospital Stays If you’re hospitalized for less than 24 hours for a covered vector-borne disease, the policy won’t provide financial assistance. However, there’s an exception for certain countries with advanced medical systems.
3 Early Diagnosis Exclusion The mos bite insurance policy excludes coverage for vector-borne diseases diagnosed within the first 15 days of purchasing a new policy. Renewals are treated differently. Meaning, if you renew an existing policy with no claims in the previous year, you’ll be covered from day one.
4 Pre-Existing Conditions A 60-day waiting period applies for any pre-existing conditions you received treatment for before getting the mosquito bite care policy. Renewals with a previous claim also have a waiting period but only for the specific condition you claimed for previously.
5 Renewals with Recent Claims If you renew your policy within 60 days of making a claim, there’s a 60-day waiting period for that specific illness again. However, other covered diseases are not affected.
6 Renewals After Long Gaps Renewing your policy more than 60 days after a claim will trigger a fresh 15-day waiting period for all covered diseases. While most mos bite insurance companies go with this rule, it may vary with some.
