Term Life Insurance: Protecting Your Loved Ones

Term life insurance provides coverage for a specific period, offering financial protection to your family in the event of your untimely death, ensuring their financial stability and peace of mind.

What is Term Insurance?

Term insurance is a type of life insurance policy that provides financial coverage for a specific period, known as the “term.” During this term, if the life assured passes away due to any unfortunate event, the designated beneficiary will receive the financial protection provided by the policy. The term insurance plan offers life insurance coverage against a fixed premium paid by the insured for the specified duration.

One of the significant advantages of a term insurance plan is that it offers high life coverage at affordable premiums compared to other life insurance policies. For instance, a premium as low as Rs. 411 per month can provide a coverage of Rs. 1 Crore. This makes term insurance an excellent choice for those seeking substantial protection for their loved ones at a reasonable cost. Additionally, term insurance can serve as a financial tool to fulfill future needs, such as funding a child’s education or marriage.

It is essential to note that if the life insured survives the entire policy term, the coverage at the earlier rate of premiums is not guaranteed upon expiration. The policyholder can opt for extended coverage with different payment conditions, if available, or choose to forgo the coverage entirely. Term insurance plans bought from a reputed online insurance provider like Aapka Policywala provide a valuable safety net for individuals and their families, ensuring financial security during the policy term and peace of mind for the future.

Types of Term Insurance Plans

Features Benefits
Level Term Insurance This is the most straightforward type of term insurance, where the sum assured remains constant throughout the policy term. In case of the insured’s demise during the policy period, the designated beneficiaries receive the fixed death benefit.
Decreasing Term Insurance   In this type of plan, the sum assured decreases over time at a predefined rate. Decreasing term insurance is often chosen to cover specific liabilities like home loans, where the outstanding loan amount decreases over time. It provides a more affordable option for coverage.
Increasing Term Insurance Unlike decreasing term insurance, increasing term insurance offers a rising sum assured over the policy term. This helps counter inflation and ensures that the policy’s coverage keeps pace with the increasing cost of living.
Convertible Term Insurance Convertible term plans offer the flexibility to convert the term policy into a permanent life insurance policy, such as whole life or endowment insurance, without the need for a medical examination. This allows the policyholder to adapt to changing financial needs and goals.
Term Insurance with Return of Premium (TROP) TROP plans provide the option to receive the total premium paid during the policy term as a maturity benefit if the insured survives the policy period. If the insured dies during the term, the death benefit is paid to the beneficiaries as per the policy terms.
Term Insurance with Riders Policyholders can enhance their term insurance coverage by adding riders or additional benefits. Common riders include critical illness cover, accidental death benefit, waiver of premium, and disability cover.
Joint Term Insurance This type of term plan covers multiple individuals under a single policy, usually spouses or business partners. The death benefit is paid when either of the insured persons passes away.
Group Term Insurance Group term plans are designed to cover a group of people, such as employees of a company or members of an organization. These policies offer cost-effective coverage to a large number of individuals under a single master policy.

Govt Term Insurance Schemes

In order to protect its residents and to provide for the future of the insured’s dependents or family members in the terrible event of his untimely passing, the government of India has also developed various term insurance programmes.

Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana (PMJJBY): Launched by the Government of India, PMJJBY is a life insurance scheme for individuals between the ages of 18 and 50 years. It provides a renewable one-year term insurance coverage with a sum assured of Rs. 2 lakhs. The premium for PMJJBY is subsidized and affordable, making it accessible to low-income individuals.

Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY): PMSBY is another government-backed insurance scheme aimed at providing accidental death and disability coverage. It offers a one-year term insurance cover with a sum assured of Rs. 2 lakhs for accidental death and permanent total disability. The premium is minimal, making it accessible to a large number of people.

Aam Aadmi Bima Yojana (AABY): AABY is a social security scheme targeted at rural landless households. It offers life insurance and disability coverage to the primary breadwinner of the family. The scheme also includes natural death coverage along with partial disability coverage.

Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana (RSBY): While not a pure term insurance scheme, RSBY is a government-sponsored health insurance program that provides health coverage to below-poverty-line families. It offers a predefined health insurance cover for hospitalization expenses.

Factors That Influence the Cost of Term Insurance

The premium for a term insurance policy is determined based on various factors that assess the risk associated with providing life coverage to the insured. Some of the key factors that affect term insurance premiums include:

Age

Age is a significant factor in determining the premium. Younger individuals typically pay lower premiums as they are considered less prone to health risks compared to older individuals.

Sum Assured

The sum assured or the coverage amount chosen by the policyholder directly affects the premium. Higher coverage amounts lead to higher premiums.

Policy Term

The duration of the term insurance policy also influences the premium. Longer policy terms often result in higher premiums due to the extended coverage period.

Health Condition

The health condition of the insured plays a crucial role in determining the premium. Individuals with pre-existing medical conditions or unhealthy lifestyles may have higher premiums as they pose higher risks.

Lifestyle Habits

Habits such as smoking, alcohol consumption, or engagement in hazardous activities can impact the premium. Insurers may charge higher premiums for individuals with risky habits.

Occupation

The nature of the insured’s occupation can influence the premium amount. Risky or hazardous professions may lead to higher premiums.

Gender

On average, women tend to have lower term insurance premiums compared to men due to statistical differences in life expectancy.

Family Medical History

A family history of certain medical conditions may impact the premium as it may increase the risk for the insured.

Riders and Add-ons

Opting for additional riders or add-on benefits to enhance the policy coverage will increase the premium amount.

Policy Features

The type of term insurance policy and its features, such as level term or increasing vterm, also affect the premium.

Who Should Buy Term Insurance?

Term insurance is a valuable financial product that provides essential protection to individuals and their families. It is an ideal choice for various groups of people, including:

Breadwinners

Anyone who is the primary earning member of their family should consider purchasing term insurance. It ensures that in the unfortunate event of their demise, their family will receive financial support to meet daily expenses, pay off debts, and maintain their standard of living.

Parents

Parents with dependent children should have term insurance coverage to secure their children’s future in case of any unforeseen circumstances. The policy proceeds can be used to fund their education, marriage, and other important life events.

Young and Healthy Individuals

Young and healthy individuals can avail term insurance at relatively lower premiums. Buying term insurance early in life ensures long-term coverage and cost-effectiveness.

Married Couples

Married couples should consider term insurance to protect each other financially. In the event of the death of one spouse, the surviving spouse can utilize the policy proceeds to maintain their lifestyle and achieve financial goals.

Debt-Holders

Individuals with significant financial liabilities, such as home loans, car loans, or personal loans, should buy term insurance to ensure that their family does not face the burden of repaying these debts in case of their demise.

Business Owners

Business owners should consider term insurance to safeguard their businesses and protect their family’s financial interests. It can be used to cover business loans and ensure the continuity of the business in case of the owner’s untimely death.

Single Parents

Single parents are the sole providers for their children and need to secure their children’s future. Term insurance offers a cost-effective way to ensure financial stability for their dependents.

Retirees

Though term insurance is more commonly purchased by younger individuals, some term policies can be extended into retirement years. Retirees who have financial dependents or outstanding debts may find value in having term insurance coverage during their retirement phase.

How Do I Make a Term Insurance Policy Claim?

To claim for a term insurance policy, follow these steps:

Inform the Insurer: In the event of the policyholder’s death or any other claimable event, inform the insurance company as soon as possible. Contact their customer service or claims department to initiate the claim process.

Provide Necessary Documents: The insurer will require certain documents to process the claim. These may include the original policy document, death certificate (in case of death claim), medical records, and any other relevant documents as per the claim event.

Claim Form Submission: Complete the claim form provided by the insurance company. You can get the form from their website or visit their branch.Ensure that you provide accurate and complete information in the form.

Medical Records and Investigation (if applicable): If the claim event is related to a critical illness or disability, the insurer may request medical records or conduct an investigation to verify the claim.

Nominee Details: If the policy has a nominee, ensure that the nominee’s details are provided correctly. The nominee is the person who will receive the claim amount.

Settlement Process: The insurance company will evaluate the claim and verify all the submitted documents. If everything is in order and the claim is genuine, they will process the claim settlement.

Claim Amount Disbursement: Once the claim is approved, the insurance company will disburse the claim amount to the nominee or the beneficiary as per the policy terms.

Claim Settlement Timeframe: The claim settlement process typically takes a few weeks, depending on the complexity of the case and the submission of all required documents.

Disputed Claims: In case of a dispute or rejection of the claim, the policyholder or the nominee can approach the insurance company’s grievance redressal department. If the issue remains unresolved, they can escalate the matter to the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI).

To ensure a smooth claim settlement process:

  • Keep the policy document and other relevant documents easily accessible to the nominee or beneficiary.
  • Keep the insurer informed about any changes in your contact information or address.
  • Pay the premiums regularly to keep the policy in force.
  • Disclose all relevant information honestly at the time of purchasing the policy to avoid claim rejection due to non-disclosure.
