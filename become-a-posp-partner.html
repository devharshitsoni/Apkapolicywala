Who is a POSP?

POSP (Point of Sales Person) refers to an individual who acts as a sales agent or intermediary for insurance products and services. They play a crucial role in the distribution and sale of insurance policies to customers. POSPs are authorized by insurance companies or intermediaries to sell insurance products directly to customers at the point of sale. The concept of POSP was introduced to extend the reach of insurance services and make them more accessible to customers, especially in areas where traditional insurance agents might not be readily available. POSPs often operate on a commission-based model, earning a commission on the insurance policies they sell.

Key characteristics of a POSP:

Direct Sales: POSPs engage in direct sales of insurance products to customers. They may approach potential clients, offer insurance solutions, explain policy details, and facilitate the purchase of insurance plans.

Product Knowledge: POSPs are required to have a good understanding of the insurance products they are selling. They should be able to explain policy features, benefits, terms, and conditions to customers.

Training and Certification: In many jurisdictions, POSPs are required to undergo training and obtain certification from insurance regulators or companies to ensure they are well-informed about the insurance industry and adhere to ethical practices.

Digital Enablement: Many POSPs leverage digital platforms and technology to facilitate the sales process. This includes using online portals or mobile applications to provide quotes, complete applications, and issue insurance policies.

Distribution Channels: POSPs may operate through various distribution channels, such as insurance company branches, banks, online platforms, or insurance aggregators.

What kind of goods can be offered through POSP?

A POSP Partner can sell the most important vital policies by analysing the vast scope and potential that are there in the current market.

Key characteristics of a POSP:

Life Insurance: POSPs can sell various types of life insurance policies, including term insurance, whole life insurance, endowment plans, and unit-linked insurance plans (ULIPs). These policies provide financial protection and investment opportunities to policyholders.

Health Insurance: POSPs can offer health insurance plans that cover medical expenses, hospitalization costs, and other related medical treatments. Health insurance policies may include individual health plans, family floater policies, and critical illness insurance.

Motor Insurance: POSPs can sell motor insurance policies that provide coverage for vehicles against damages, theft, and third-party liabilities. These policies include car insurance, two-wheeler insurance, and commercial vehicle insurance.

Travel Insurance: POSPs can offer travel insurance policies that provide coverage for trip cancellations, lost baggage, medical emergencies, and other travel-related risks.

Home Insurance: POSPs may sell home insurance policies that protect homes and their contents against damages caused by fire, theft, natural disasters, and other perils.

Personal Accident Insurance: POSPs can offer personal accident insurance that provides coverage for accidental death, disability, and related medical expenses.

Rural and Micro-Insurance: POSPs may also sell rural insurance products and micro-insurance plans tailored to the specific needs of customers in rural or low-income areas.
