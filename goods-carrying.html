What's Not Covered in Commercial Vehicle Insurance

Being aware of what’s not covered in your commercial vehicle insurance policy is as important as understanding what is covered. It will help avoid surprises and provide clarity when filing a claim for your damaged or stolen truck, bus, school van, pickup truck, or auto rickshaw. Here are some situations where coverage may not apply:

Own Damages for Third-Party Policy Holder

Under a third-party liability only policy, damages to your own vehicle will not be covered. It's essential to consider upgrading your policy for comprehensive coverage.

Drunk Riding or Without a Licence

Coverage may be denied if the owner driver is found to be under the influence of alcohol or driving without a valid license. Adhering to Indian traffic regulations is paramount to ensure coverage.

Contributory Negligence

Any damage resulting from the driver's contributory negligence such as driving without timely rest or during an undergoing natural calamity may not be covered. Remember, practicing safe driving habits is key to avoiding such situations!

Consequential Damages

Consequential losses such as loss of earnings and market value which are not direct results of the accident or natural calamity may not be covered. Make sure to understand the scope of coverage to manage expectations effectively.
